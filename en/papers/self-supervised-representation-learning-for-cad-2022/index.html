Self-Supervised Representation Learning for CAD

arXiv 2022 49.3 method

TLDR

Proposes self-supervised representation learning for CAD B-Rep geometry using a hybrid implicit/explicit surface, improving few-shot learning and achieving SOTA on benchmarks.

Reasoning

Strengths include a novel hybrid representation and effective use of unlabeled data, with strong empirical results on B-Rep benchmarks. Weaknesses are the narrow focus on B-Rep and lack of generative or multi-modal tasks, limiting broader applicability.

Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Self-supervised pre-training on unlabeled CAD B-Rep geometry
  • Novel hybrid implicit/explicit surface representation for B-Rep

Methodology

The method pre-trains a model on unlabeled CAD geometry using a hybrid implicit/explicit surface representation for boundary representation (B-Rep) data, then fine-tunes on downstream supervised tasks.

Key Results

Pre-training significantly improves few-shot learning performance and achieves state-of-the-art results on several B-Rep benchmarks.
