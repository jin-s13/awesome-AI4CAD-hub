LLM4CAD: Multi-Modal Large Language Models for 3D Computer-Aided Design Generation

arXiv 2024 49.4 method

TLDR

Explores using multimodal LLMs (GPT-4, GPT-4V) to generate 3D CAD models via CAD programs, with quantitative evaluation on syntactic correctness and shape accuracy.

Reasoning

Strengths include a novel application of LLMs to CAD generation, a data synthesis pipeline, and quantitative metrics (IoU). Weaknesses are limited to two LLMs, potential dataset size constraints, and lack of comparison to traditional CAD generation methods.

Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • LLM4CAD approach enabling GPT-4 and GPT-4V to generate 3D CAD models via CAD program as an intermediate bridge
  • Data synthesis pipeline for generating CAD models, sketches, images, and natural-language descriptions with dimensional information for mechanical components
  • Quantitative evaluation framework measuring syntactic correctness of CAD programs and 3D shape accuracy via Intersection over Union

Methodology

The study uses GPT-4 and GPT-4V to generate CAD programs from text or multimodal inputs (text+image). A data synthesis pipeline creates CAD models, sketches, and images of mechanical components (gears, springs), with natural-language descriptions collected via Amazon Mechanical Turk. Performance is evaluated on syntactic correctness (Cap1) and 3D shape accuracy measured by Intersection over Union (Cap2).

Key Results

GPT-4V with text-only input outperformed multimodal inputs on average for both syntactic correctness and shape accuracy, but multimodal inputs showed greater benefit for complex geometries like springs and gears in syntactic correctness.

Limitations

  • Multimodal input does not universally improve performance; its benefit depends on the complexity of the target CAD geometry
  • Evaluation limited to a small set of mechanical component categories (gears, springs) and synthetic data
