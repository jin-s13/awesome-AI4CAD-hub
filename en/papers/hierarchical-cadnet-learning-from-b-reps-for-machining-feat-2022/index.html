Hierarchical CADNet: Learning from B-Reps for Machining Feature Recognition

arXiv 2022 31.2 method, application

TLDR

A hierarchical graph convolutional network (Hierarchical CADNet) learns from B-Reps to recognize machining features, especially intersecting ones, outperforming prior methods.

Reasoning

The paper introduces a novel hierarchical B-Rep graph representation and a dedicated network that effectively handles intersecting features, a known weakness in prior work. Strengths include explicit use of geometric and topological information; however, the abstract lacks details on dataset size, real-world benchmarks, or generalizability beyond machining features.

Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Novel hierarchical B-Rep graph shape representation encoding surface geometry and face topology
  • Hierarchical graph convolutional network (Hierarchical CADNet) designed to learn from the hierarchical B-Rep graph

Methodology

The paper proposes a hierarchical graph representation of B-Rep data that captures surface geometry and face topology, and a corresponding hierarchical graph convolutional network (Hierarchical CADNet) to perform machining feature recognition. The model is compared against state-of-the-art neural architectures on feature identification tasks, including intersecting features.

Key Results

Hierarchical CADNet outperforms other state-of-the-art neural architectures on machining feature recognition, particularly for intersecting features, with accuracy improvements on more complex CAD models.
