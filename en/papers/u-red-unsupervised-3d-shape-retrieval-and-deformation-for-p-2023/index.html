U-RED: Unsupervised 3D Shape Retrieval and Deformation for Partial Point Clouds

arXiv 2023 42.6 method, system

TLDR

U-RED retrieves and deforms CAD models from a database to match noisy partial point clouds using a sphere-based one-to-many mapping and noise-robust metric.

Reasoning

The paper introduces a novel unsupervised pipeline for shape retrieval and deformation that handles ambiguous partial observations and noise, achieving significant improvements on synthetic and real-world datasets. However, it relies on a pre-existing CAD database and does not generate new CAD models, limiting its applicability to generative tasks.

Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Unsupervised joint shape retrieval and deformation pipeline for partial point clouds
  • Learning to project all possible full shapes of a partial target onto a unit sphere surface to handle one-to-many ambiguity, with sampling-based retrieval
  • Point-wise residual-guided metric for noise-robust shape similarity comparison

Methodology

U-RED takes an object observation (RGB images or scans) and jointly retrieves and deforms CAD models from a pre-established database. It addresses partial-to-full ambiguity by mapping possible full shapes to a unit sphere, enabling sampling-based retrieval, and uses a point-wise residual-guided metric for noise-robust comparison.

Key Results

U-RED surpasses existing state-of-the-art approaches by 47.3%, 16.7% and 31.6% on PartNet, ComplementMe, and Scan2CAD respectively under Chamfer Distance.
