Leveraging Geometry for Shape Estimation from a Single RGB Image

arXiv 2021 44.2 method

TLDR

Improves 3D shape and pose estimation from single RGB images by leveraging geometry via keypoint matches to retrieve and adapt CAD models.

Reasoning

Strengths: Novel use of cross-domain keypoint matches for both pose and shape adaptation, achieving significant improvements on Pix3D dataset. Weaknesses: Limited to retrieval-based approach; no generative or parametric modeling; evaluation only on one dataset.

Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Cross-domain keypoint matches between RGB image and rendered CAD model for precise object pose prediction
  • Shape adaptation using keypoint matches to modify retrieved CAD model shape beyond database limitations
  • Geometric shape prediction that improves accuracy for both seen and unseen objects

Methodology

The method retrieves CAD models from large-scale databases and aligns them to objects in the image using cross-domain keypoint matches between the RGB image and a rendered CAD model. These keypoint matches are used to estimate object pose and to adapt the shape of the retrieved CAD model, bridging the gap to the observed shape. Evaluation is performed on the Pix3D dataset.

Key Results

The proposed geometric shape prediction improves AP mesh over state-of-the-art from 33.2 to 37.8 on seen objects and from 8.2 to 17.1 on unseen objects, and shape adaptation yields more accurate predictions without closely matching CAD models.
